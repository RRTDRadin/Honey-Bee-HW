Honey Bee



Bees are fascinating insects known for their crucial role in pollination and honey production. They belong to the order Hymenoptera and are typically characterized by their fuzzy bodies, which are covered in tiny hairs that help collect pollen. There are over 20,000 species of bees, with honeybees and bumblebees being among the most well-known.

    Here are key points about bees:
  1. Classification: Belong to the order Hymenoptera, family Apidae.
  2. Types: Common types include honeybees, bumblebees, and solitary bees.
  3. Physical Features:
    • Robust bodies covered in fine hairs for pollen collection.
    • Two pairs of wings.
    • Compound eyes for navigation.
    • Specialized mouthparts for nectar feeding.
